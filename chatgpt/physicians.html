ChatGPT for Physicians: An Honest Review (2026)

Where ChatGPT genuinely reduces physician administrative burden, the HIPAA compliance distinction most guides get wrong, and the workflows that save the most time. Plus prompts for SOAP notes, prior auth letters, and patient-facing summaries.

Recommended March 19, 2026 7 min read

By Richard Migliorisi · Fact-checked by Ryan Cooper · March 19, 2026

Bottom Line

ChatGPT is the most practical AI tool for reducing physician administrative burden in 2026. Documentation drafting, prior authorization letters, after-visit summaries, and patient-facing explanations are all tasks where it saves 30 to 60 minutes per day with minimal learning curve. Critical caveat: consumer ChatGPT (Free and Plus) does not include a HIPAA BAA. Never enter patient-identifiable information into a non-BAA account. ChatGPT Team and Enterprise offer BAAs, but verify terms with OpenAI before use on covered data.

KEY TAKEAWAY

Physicians spend 2 to 3 hours per day on documentation and administrative tasks outside direct patient care. These tasks are largely mechanical formatting and communication work that ChatGPT handles well. The limiting factor is not AI capability but compliance: consumer ChatGPT accounts lack a HIPAA BAA and must not be used with patient information.

BEST USE CASES

  • SOAP notes and H&P formatting from encounter descriptions
  • Prior authorization letter drafts
  • After-visit summaries in plain patient language
  • Referral letters and specialist handoff notes

AVOID IF

  • You need to enter patient-identifiable information without a BAA
  • You need real-time transcription of patient encounters
  • You need cited, current medical literature sources
  • You expect it to replace clinical judgment on diagnosis or treatment

MINI WORKFLOW

After the patient encounter, describe what occurred in natural language (with no patient-identifiable information if using a non-BAA account). Ask ChatGPT to format it as a SOAP note. Review the output and correct any clinical inaccuracies before entry. For prior auth letters, describe the clinical indication and requested service; ChatGPT drafts the supporting letter. Review before submitting to payers.

What ChatGPT Does Well for Physicians

The most important framing: ChatGPT is a documentation and communication tool for physicians, not a clinical decision support system. Its value is in reducing the administrative work that consumes a significant portion of every physician's day. The documentation burden, the prior authorization process, the patient communication load, and the referral letter volume are all tasks where ChatGPT delivers measurable time savings without requiring clinical judgment from the AI.

SOAP Note and Clinical Documentation Formatting

A physician dictates or types a natural-language description of the patient encounter. ChatGPT formats it into a structured SOAP note (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan), H&P, or discharge summary with the correct section headings and clinical structure. The physician reviews and corrects the output, which is faster than writing from scratch. For de-identified or hypothetical encounters used in template building, even consumer ChatGPT is appropriate. For encounters with real patient data, a BAA-compliant account is required.

Prior Authorization Letters

Prior authorization requests are one of the most time-consuming and low-value administrative tasks in medicine. ChatGPT can draft a complete prior authorization letter given the clinical indication, requested service or medication, patient diagnosis, relevant clinical history, and payer name. The output requires physician review for accuracy and to ensure it reflects actual clinical circumstances, but the drafting time drops from 20 minutes to 2 to 3 minutes. Physicians report this as one of the highest-ROI ChatGPT use cases.

Patient-Facing After-Visit Summaries

Clinical documentation written for the medical record is not written for patients. ChatGPT can translate a clinical summary into patient-friendly language at an appropriate reading level, replacing medical terminology with plain English equivalents, and organizing the information as action items the patient needs to follow. This produces more useful patient education materials than most EHR-generated summaries and takes under a minute.

Referral and Specialist Handoff Letters

Referral letters require organizing clinical background, current presentation, reason for referral, relevant test results, and the specific clinical question for the specialist. ChatGPT structures this efficiently given the clinical inputs. The physician fills in clinical specifics; ChatGPT provides the letter scaffolding, professional tone, and appropriate structure. This is particularly useful for high-volume primary care practices where referral letter volume is substantial.

Prompts to Try

These prompts use de-identified or template placeholders. For any prompt involving actual patient data, use only a HIPAA BAA-compliant ChatGPT account. Review all output for clinical accuracy before use.

Please format the following clinical encounter description as a structured SOAP note with proper section headings (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan). Use standard clinical terminology. Keep the Assessment concise with a differential if appropriate. Keep the Plan as numbered action items with appropriate follow-up timeframes.

[PASTE ENCOUNTER DESCRIPTION HERE. DE-IDENTIFIED OR IN BAA-COMPLIANT ACCOUNT ONLY]

Draft prior authorization letter

Draft a prior authorization letter to [PAYER NAME] requesting approval for [REQUESTED SERVICE/MEDICATION]. Clinical indication: [DIAGNOSIS AND RELEVANT CLINICAL HISTORY]. Supporting documentation available: [LIST RELEVANT LABS/IMAGING/NOTES]. The letter should be professional, cite clinical necessity clearly, and follow standard prior authorization letter structure. Flag any section I need to fill in with specific clinical data.

Patient-friendly after-visit summary

Rewrite this clinical summary as a patient-friendly after-visit summary at a Grade 7 reading level. Replace all medical terminology with plain English. Organize as: (1) What we discussed today, (2) Your diagnosis or the reason for your visit, (3) What to do next (action items with specific instructions), (4) When to contact us or seek urgent care, (5) Follow-up appointment information.

Where ChatGPT Falls Short for Physicians

✗

Consumer plans have no HIPAA BAA

ChatGPT Free and Plus do not include a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement. Any physician entering patient-identifiable information into these accounts is in violation of HIPAA regardless of the clinical purpose. ChatGPT Team and Enterprise plans offer BAAs. Verify current BAA availability and terms with OpenAI before using any ChatGPT account with covered health information.

✗

Does not search current medical literature

ChatGPT's knowledge has a training cutoff and it does not search PubMed, UpToDate, or current clinical guidelines. For questions about recent drug approvals, updated screening guidelines, or rare presentations, ChatGPT can produce plausible but outdated or incorrect information. For cited, current literature, use Perplexity AI. Never use ChatGPT as a substitute for evidence-based clinical resources.

✗

Not a real-time transcription tool

ChatGPT does not transcribe live patient encounters. It formats text you provide after the encounter. For real-time encounter transcription, use Otter.ai or a dedicated ambient documentation tool. The strongest documentation workflow combines both: Otter.ai captures the spoken encounter, ChatGPT formats the transcript into a structured clinical document.

✗

All clinical output requires physician review

ChatGPT can produce clinically plausible but inaccurate formatting, incorrect dosages, or wrong clinical terminology in documentation it generates. Every piece of clinical content produced by ChatGPT requires review by the responsible physician before use in any patient record, letter, or communication. This is not optional; it is a professional and legal obligation.

HIPAA Compliance Warning

Do not enter any patient-identifiable information (name, date of birth, MRN, dates of service, geographic identifiers, or any other PHI) into consumer ChatGPT (Free or Plus plans). These accounts do not have a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement. ChatGPT Team and Enterprise plans offer BAAs, but confirm current terms with OpenAI and your compliance officer before processing patient data. Your institution may also have its own AI use policy that restricts use further.

Common Questions

Answers start with Yes, No, or It depends.

Is ChatGPT HIPAA compliant for physicians?

It depends on the plan. Consumer ChatGPT (Free and Plus) does not include a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement and must not be used with any patient-identifiable information. ChatGPT Team and Enterprise plans offer BAAs. Confirm current BAA availability with OpenAI and review your organization's compliance policy before using any ChatGPT account with covered health data. Your institution may have additional restrictions.

What is the best use of ChatGPT for physicians?

Yes, several tasks stand out. Documentation drafting (SOAP notes, H&Ps, discharge summaries) and prior authorization letters are the two highest-ROI uses. After-visit summaries in plain patient language and referral letters are close behind. These are all mechanical formatting and communication tasks that take significant physician time but require no clinical judgment from the AI. ChatGPT handles the structure and language; the physician provides and verifies the clinical substance.

Can ChatGPT help physicians write SOAP notes?

Yes. ChatGPT can take a physician's natural-language encounter description and format it as a structured SOAP note with correct section headings and clinical language. The physician provides the clinical content; ChatGPT provides the structure and formatting. Always review the output for accuracy before adding to any record. Consumer ChatGPT must not be used with actual patient-identifiable information without a HIPAA BAA.

Can ChatGPT replace medical scribes for physicians?

No, not completely. ChatGPT handles post-encounter documentation formatting, which is part of what a scribe does. For live encounter capture, pair it with Otter.ai (for real-time transcription) and then use ChatGPT to format the transcript. Scribes also manage workflow elements ChatGPT cannot replicate. The Otter.ai plus ChatGPT combination is the closest AI equivalent to scribe functionality for documentation-focused tasks.

Is ChatGPT accurate for medical information?

It depends on the question. ChatGPT has strong general medical knowledge from training but does not search current literature and does not cite sources. It can produce plausible but incorrect statements, particularly for recent drug approvals, updated screening guidelines, or uncommon conditions. Never use ChatGPT as a substitute for clinical judgment or evidence-based resources. For cited, current medical literature, use Perplexity AI.

How does ChatGPT compare to Otter.ai for physician documentation?

They serve different functions and work best as a pair. Otter.ai is a real-time transcription tool that captures the spoken encounter as it happens. ChatGPT formats and restructures that transcript into clinical document formats like SOAP notes, after-visit summaries, and letters. The most powerful physician documentation workflow uses both: Otter.ai for live capture, ChatGPT for formatting and editing the transcript into the correct document type.

WHAT MOST REVIEWS MISS

1.

The HIPAA BAA distinction is the most critical fact, and most reviews bury it or get it wrong. Articles that say "don't put patient information in ChatGPT" without explaining that Team and Enterprise plans DO have BAAs available are giving physicians incomplete guidance. A physician using ChatGPT Team with a properly executed BAA is in a fundamentally different compliance position than one using Plus. Knowing this distinction determines whether ChatGPT is viable for clinical documentation at all.

2.

Prior authorization is the most underrated use case. Reviews focus on SOAP notes because they are the obvious documentation use case. Prior authorization letters are actually where many physicians get the highest ROI. Each PA letter historically takes 20 to 30 minutes to draft. ChatGPT reduces that to 2 to 3 minutes. For a physician completing 5 to 10 prior authorizations per week, this is 2 to 5 hours of recovered time per week from a single use case.

3.

The Otter.ai plus ChatGPT combination is the most practical documentation workflow and almost no one describes it clearly. Otter.ai transcribes the encounter in real time. ChatGPT formats the transcript. Together they approximate an AI scribe without the live AI assistant listening during sensitive patient conversations. The physician reviews and edits the final output before it enters the record. This workflow is simpler than most ambient documentation platforms and uses tools physicians can adopt individually without institutional IT approval.

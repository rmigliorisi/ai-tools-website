How Finance Professionals Are Using AI Right Now

Finance professionals deal with vast amounts of text-heavy work: filings, research, reports, client communications, and regulatory documents. AI compresses the reading and writing tasks without replacing the analytical judgment that creates real value. Here is where it fits into the workflow.

RESEARCH

Earnings Call and Filing Summarization

Paste an earnings transcript or 10-K section into Claude and ask for a structured summary: key metrics, guidance changes, management commentary, and risk factors. What used to take 45 minutes takes 5. You still verify the numbers, but the orientation is done. Perplexity AI handles live research when you need current data with citations.

Try: Claude for filings, Perplexity AI for live research
CLIENT REPORTS

Commentary and Report Drafting

Give AI the portfolio data, key metrics, and market context and ask for a first draft of the quarterly letter or performance commentary. The structure and language will be there; you add the judgment and firm voice. Final output must be reviewed and approved by a licensed professional before sending to clients.

Try: ChatGPT or Claude
REGULATORY

Regulatory Document Review

Long regulatory filings, compliance memos, and rule updates are ideal candidates for AI-assisted review. Claude handles lengthy documents in a single pass. Ask it to flag material changes, summarize key provisions, or explain a complex regulatory concept in plain language for internal training.

Try: Claude for long documents, Perplexity AI for regulation tracking
DATA WORK

Excel Formula and Spreadsheet Assistance

Describe what you need a formula to do and ChatGPT or Copilot writes it. Copilot for Microsoft 365 does this directly inside Excel, which is faster. For complex model documentation, Copilot also drafts explanatory text alongside your spreadsheet. The time savings on formula troubleshooting alone adds up quickly.

Try: Microsoft Copilot in Excel, or ChatGPT for formula help

COMPLIANCE NOTICE

AI tools do not give financial advice, and any output used in regulated communications must be reviewed and approved by a licensed professional. Consumer-tier AI tools are generally not appropriate for workflows involving non-public information or client account data. Confirm with your compliance officer which tools and use cases are permitted at your firm before deploying in client-facing workflows.

Common Questions

What professionals ask before adopting AI tools.

Can AI tools provide financial advice?

No. AI tools are research and drafting assistants, not licensed financial advisors. Output from any AI tool must be reviewed, verified, and approved by a qualified professional before it is used in client communications, investment decisions, or regulatory filings. Treating AI output as advice creates liability.

Are AI tools appropriate for regulated financial workflows?

Enterprise versions with appropriate data agreements can be used in many workflows, but you must verify what your firm's compliance program permits. Consumer-tier AI tools are generally not appropriate for non-public or client-sensitive information. Consult your compliance officer before deploying AI in client-facing or regulated workflows. [VERIFY DETAILS]

Can AI summarize earnings calls and financial filings?

Yes, and this is one of the best current use cases. Paste the transcript or filing into Claude or ChatGPT and ask for a structured summary with key figures, guidance changes, and risk factors. The tool does not have real-time market data, so combine this with a live data source for context.

Which tool is best for analyzing financial documents?

Claude is the strongest for long-document analysis due to its 200K-token context window, which can fit most earnings transcripts, prospectuses, and regulatory filings. Perplexity AI is better for real-time research with source citations. Use both for different parts of the workflow.

Can AI help write investment thesis memos?

Yes. Provide the key thesis, supporting data, and counterarguments and ChatGPT or Claude will structure a coherent investment memo with appropriate sections. The draft requires your analytical judgment to be accurate, but it saves significant time on formatting and prose. Never submit AI-assisted investment memos without thorough human review and verification of all claims and figures.

How do I use AI for client communications without creating liability?

Use AI to draft the structure and language, then review every factual claim before sending. Avoid having AI make specific investment recommendations or performance predictions. Add your firm's standard disclosures and compliance language. Have a licensed professional review and approve all client-facing materials before they go out, regardless of whether AI assisted in drafting.
